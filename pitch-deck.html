Why Only Tide Makes This Possible
Two primitives no competitor can replicate.
Tide PrimitiveWhat We Use It ForWhat Breaks Without Tide
doEncrypt / doDecrypt Seal document content under the signer's identity. The platform literally cannot read what you sign. Zero key handling — the user never sees or manages a key. Without Tide self-encryption, we'd need to manage keys ourselves — making us a custodian. The entire "platform can't read your data" pitch collapses.
DPoP-bound tokens Every signing session is cryptographically bound to the device. A stolen token is inert elsewhere — critical for high-stakes signatures like real estate closings. Without DPoP, token theft = forged signatures. A stolen session cookie could sign documents as you. DPoP makes token exfiltration a non-event.
Forseti contracts (mocked) Signatures only execute if the contract validates identity, role, and document hash. Policy enforced at the ORK network level, not in app code. Without Forseti, we'd be enforcing signing policy in our own code — making us the very custodian we're trying to eliminate.
